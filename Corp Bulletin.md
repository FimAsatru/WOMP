# Welcome, new citizen, to the WOMPire!
In this document, we will present all the information you need to find your way around your new home. Should you have questions about any of the points presented here, feel free to ask them in the **#newbros** channel on Discord.

## Home System
Our current staging system is **3-FKCZ** with our home station, the **3-FKCZ - W O M P S T A R**. This is the starting point for the majority of our PVP fleets and is also our local market hub. 

### I just landed in the **W O M P S T A R**, what can I do?
First things first, once you're docked in the **W O M P S T A R**, make sure to right click its name in the top left and set it as your Home Station! From there, your next steps depend on whether you want to make some money or do some shooting:

#### PvP Activities
- Join Standing Fleet.  
  You can find the standing fleet in your Fleet Finder as "WOMP Standing". Standing fleet should be up most of the time and you can bring any ship you are willing to lose. Makre sure to also join Standing Comms on Mumble while in fleet! If you're doing a PvE activity in our space, you should strongly consider joining standing fleet as well as Standing Comms on Mumble; both to get help should you get engaged by hostiles as well as hang out with your fellow womplings. Standing fleet losses are not SRP'd by the alliance. 
- Join a content fleet.  
  Content fleets can be pinged by anyone in the alliance, they go into the **#content-pings** channel on Discord. They are for-fun fleets with no strategic objective and often don't use alliance doctrine-fit ships. Content fleets are not SRP'd by the alliance.
- Join a Stratop.  
  There are two types of strategically important fleets to the alliance, Stratop and CTA (**C**all **T**o **A**rms). When a Stratop gets pinged, you should strongly consider joining it. When a CTA gets pinged, you should stop doing whatever other PvE/PvP activity you're doing and join the CTA. Stratops and CTAs will be pinged in the **#strat-pings-querious** channel on Discord and will almost always use alliance doctrine fits. Losses during Stratops **are** SRP'd by the alliance.

#### Acquiring Ships and Items
##### Free Ships!
If you can't afford a PvP ship (either doctrine or otherwise), you can ask for a handout frigate from TWATs in the **#newbros** channel on Discord. You can get either a **Tackle Merlin**, a **Tackle Slasher**, or an **EWAR Vigil**. The ships are free to replace, should you lose one, just ask for another one.

##### Alliance Contracts
You can find most of our doctrine ships we use in Stratops on Alliance Contract. To find those contracts, go to your *Neocom* -> *Finance* -> *Contracts*. Switch to the *Contract Search* tab. In the *Item Type* field, write whatever ship your looking for or leave it blank to see all contracts. Set *Location* to *Current Station*. Then, further below, set *Availability* to *My Alliance*. Finally, click *Search* to get a list of the available Alliance Contracts.

##### Market
The **W O M P S T A R** is our main marketplace, you can find plenty of items listed there. 

##### Shipping
Should the market be missing an item you are looking for, you can have it shipped in by one of our shipping services. To do so, buy what you need in **Jita**, then go to the [Freight Calculator](https://auth.wompa.space/freight/calculator) on Auth. Set the *Route* to *Jita <-> 3-FKCZ*, the *Volume* to however large your item(s) is/are, and the *Collateral* to however much you paid for your items. Then click the button labeled "Click To Calculate Reward!". Switch back to the game, select all the items you want delivered, then right-click them and select *Create Contract*. Switch the *Contract Type* to *Courier*, fill the rest out with the information you find on the Freight Calculator's site under *Your contract*. 
Courier contracts are fulfilled by real humans, so the delivery time will vary based on their availability.

## Auth
There's a good chance you have already interacted with our [Website](https://auth.wompa.space) before joining; however, in this section, we will be taking a closer look at the things you can do on "Auth" (as we usually refer to our website), now that you are a full member of WOMP.

1. **Dashboard**  
   The Dashboard gives you a quick overview over all characters you have authed with us as well as an overview over all groups you are a member of.
2. **Groups** and **Secure Groups**  
   Here you can join various groups within WOMP. Some groups have a description, others don't; if they don't, the name of the group usually speaks for itself. If you're unsure, you can see if the group has a Leader and DM them, or ask in the **#newbros** channel on Discord. If the button at the end of the group's line says "Join", you can just click that and will become a member of that group immediately. If it says "Request", then the Leader of the group has to approve your application to that group before you can join it. The application process is handled by real humans, so the response time will vary based on their availability.
3. **Services**  
   Under **Services**, you can connect to our Discord and our Mumble. You probably already have connected to our Discord during your application process; if not, you can do so here. You should also join our Mumble, a detailed explanation on how to do that as well as how to set up your Whisper/Shout keys can be found above.
4. **Applications**  
   This is the section where you put in your application to join the WOMPire, specifically the executor corp, **[GUNS-]**. 
5. **Buyback Program**  
   Here you can find buyback programs set up by members of the alliance. You can see the name of the buyback, at which location(s) they accept your contracts, how much tax they apply to your items, who runs/manages the buyback, as well as various icons depicting various settings for this specific buyback. You can hover over those items to get more information about them. At the end of each buyback, there might be a button you can click to see if that buyback has any "Special Taxes" for different items/item groups, as well as a button to actually "Use" that buyback. If you click "Use", you will go to a new site where you can enter all the items you would like to sell to that buyback, an optional donation, as well as additional notes you might want to convey to the buyback manager. Once you click the "Calculate" button at the bottom, you will see how much you will get for your items and where to contract them to. You can then use that information to actually fill out the buyback contract ingame.  
   You can find additional information by clicking on the "FAQ" button in the top left of the Buyback Program page.  
   Should you wish to set up your own buyback, you can also do so here, using the buttons in the top right, starting with "1. Setup Manager". Should you have questions about that process, feel free to ask in the **#newbros** channel on Discord.
6. **Fittings and Doctrines**  
   In this section you can find all official alliance doctrines and the appropriate fittings for the ships therein. Each doctrine has two numbers in front of them. The first number denotes whether the doctrine contains **(1)** subcaps sued in our home region, **(2)** capitals, or **(3)** ships used during deployment. The second number is used for ordering purposes only. When you click on one of the doctrines, you can see which ships constitute that doctrine. You can further click on one of the ships to see the specific fitting used for that ship in the doctrine. In the top right of the Fitting Details, you can click one of three buttons. "Save to EVE" will allow you to directly save that fit to one of your characters' personal fittings ingame. "Copy Buy All" will put all the modules of the fitting as well as the ship itself into your clipboard so you can use it in the Multibuy window ingame. "Copy Fit (EFT)" will open a pop up in which you can see the fitting in Eve's own fitting format. You can use that to import the fit manually ingame.
   By default, the **Fittings and Doctrines** page is set to "View All Doctrines". You can also show all fittings in one list by clicking "View All Fittings" in the top left.
7. **Member Audit**  
   Most likely, the **Member Audit** page will already be somewhat familiar to you, because you put in the ESIs for your characters here during the application process. Should you not have done so already, you should do so now!  
   To add a new character, locate the blank character picture with "Register Character" above it and click the "Register" button below it. On the next page, click on the button with the EVE icon and the text "LOG IN with EVE Online". You will be forwarded to the EVE Online website where you log in with the account the character you want to add is on, then select said character and click "Authorize".  
   Once you have added a character to the **Member Audit** page, you can click the green **>]** button below their potrait to get a detailed view of that character. Most of the things here are self-explanatory. One of the sections is particularly useful, though, so we'll highlight it here. When you click on the "Skills" tab, a drop down menu will open. In that menu, select "Skill Sets". Here you can check which skills your character needs to competently fly our capital and super capital fits, and subsequently get access to our Capitals and Supers groups. 
8. **Ratting Tax**  
   Should you have an alt corp in our non-wardeccable alt-alliance **Weapons of Mass Logistics**, you need to add a Corp Token here. To do so, click on the "Connect Corp Token" button in the top right, click on the button with the EVE icon and the text "LOG IN with EVE Online", then follow the same steps as when you add a new character to the **Member Audit**. Once you're done, your alt corp should show up in the "Ratting Tax Summary" table.
9. **Ship Replacement**  
   The Ship Replacement Program (**SRP**) offers members of the alliance compensation for ships lost during Stratops. Unlike most other alliances, WOMP offers SRP at contract price, not just zKillboard value! There usually is only one entry here, for the current month. To request SRP, click on the green icon with the outstretched hand under "Actions". Then, go to zkillboard.com and use the search bar in the top right to search for your character who lost the ship. Once you found the loss, copy its URL and enter it on the "Request SRP" page you previously opened. Next, provide additional information for that loss in the text box below the URL, like "Who was the FC, what doctrine was called, have changes to the fit been requested and so on." Finally, click the "Submit SRP request" button. Once your SRP request has been processed, the ISK will show up in the ingame wallet of the character who lost the ship. The SRP process is handled by real humans, so the response time will vary based on their availability.  
   You can also check the status of your SRP requests by clicking "My SRP Requests" in the top left of the **Ship Replacement** page.
10. **WOMT Freight**  
     Here you can commission a freight service to deliver items purchased by you from one location to another. For a more detailed description on how to set up a Courier contract for a freight service, see the **Shipping** section above, under **Acquiring Ships and Items**, in the **Home System** part of this document.
